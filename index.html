What is the real GDP?
real gross demostic product (real GDP) is an inflation-adjusted measure that reflects the value of the gods and services produced by an oconomy in a year and it is also reffered to as constant dollar GDP or inflation-corrected GDP.
How to calculate real GDP?
To calculate real GDP you should use the following formula:
real GDP = nominal GDP / GDP deflator
example: If the deflating number is 1.03 and the nominal GDP is $1,450,000 calculate the real GDP?
using the real GDP formula the answer will be:
real GDP = nominal GDP / GDP deflator
real GDP = $1,450,000 / 1.03
real GDP = $1,407,767
Why use real GDP?
Real GPD is important because it is used to measure the size of the economy and how it is performing. The growth rate of real GDP indicates that the economy is doing well.
